# Authority Tier Model

## Local definitions

Corpus Arbitration Layer [CAL] means the component that answers unstructured questions against LMS-Graph.

## Purpose

CAL must distinguish source authority. A sourced answer is not automatically correct, and all sources are not equal.

## Game rules authority tiers

1. Canonical source.
2. Official errata or correction.
3. Licensed supplement.
4. Adventure-specific rule.
5. Designer or publisher ruling.
6. Table-specific rule.
7. Community interpretation.
8. Generated inference.
9. Unsupported speculation.

## Scope rule

Authority is scoped. A table-specific rule is authoritative for that table. It is not authoritative for the platform. A licensed supplement may be authoritative inside a campaign that enables it. It is not universal. A designer comment may clarify intent but may not override formal errata unless configured.

## Later-domain translation

The same model generalizes to later corpora, but the authority tiers change by domain. Legal, technical, policy, medical, and compliance domains require jurisdiction, source status, date, institutional authority, and professional review.

## Human authority

CAL exposes the tier and conflict. The configured human or governance authority decides when a decision is required.
