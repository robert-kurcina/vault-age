# Technology Level Overlay and Jump Tech

## TL Vector

Technology Level is stored as a vector. Metrics include metallurgy, agriculture, warfare, medicine, transportation, communication, logistics, energy, computation, manufacturing, navigation, and social organization.

Each spatial partition has a baseline TL and metric deltas. Child partitions inherit parent constraints unless local state permits a variation.

## Baseline Progression

Baseline TL progression maps to decades or centuries. It is an Epic/World or Arc/Region phenomenon. Players can influence progression but cannot compress civilizational change into ordinary scene time.

## Jump Tech

Jump tech is localized advanced capability. It may be caused by trade contact, ruins, artifacts, inventor NPCs, faction secrecy, alien technology, magic, recovered knowledge, or plot devices.

Jump tech does not propagate automatically. It carries:

- source partition;
- affected metric;
- TL delta;
- containment scope;
- propagation lock;
- legality;
- scarcity;
- price modifier;
- decay or expiry;
- unlock conditions.

## Propagation Locks

A propagation lock prevents automatic spread. Spread requires a plot trigger, faction decision, publication event, industrialization goal, sabotage, disaster, or other explicit unlock.

## Fiction Constructor Rules

The Fiction Constructor treats jump tech as scarce, secret, valuable, illegal, mythical, or monopolized according to active flags. It does not casually normalize the tech outside its containment scope.

## Bridge Enforcement

The Bridge Layer rejects or reroutes attempts to acquire, invent, teach, manufacture, export, or normalize technology outside active TL and jump tech permissions.

## Narrative Use

Jump tech creates market advantage, contraband, espionage, monopoly, smuggling, political pressure, guild conflict, faction violence, and plot arcs.
