# Market-Selected Expansion Model

## Principle

AGE does not need to choose the first professional domain in advance. The architecture should remain domain-portable. Expansion should follow market readiness, corpus access, licensing, ROI, expert availability, and governance feasibility.

## Low-hanging fruit pattern

A good expansion corpus has:

- Bounded source roots.
- Clear users with recurring questions.
- High cost of manual research.
- Manageable licensing.
- Human authority already in the loop.
- Reviewable answers.
- Measurable correction feedback.
- Low or manageable liability.

## First non-gaming candidates

The archive should not lock one in. Plausible categories include licensed entertainment corpora, internal company policies, technical support documentation, compliance manuals, training exercise rulebooks, and other controlled corpora.

## Avoided mistake

Do not jump directly from game rules to high-stakes medicine, law, finance, or engineering without licensing, domain experts, jurisdiction modeling, audit review, and liability boundaries.
