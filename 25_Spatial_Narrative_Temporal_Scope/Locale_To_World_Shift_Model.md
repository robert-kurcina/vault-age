# Locale-to-World Shift Model

## Local definitions

Non-player character [NPC] means a character controlled by the system, Referee, or authored scenario rather than by a player.

## Definition

A locale-to-world shift is any transition where the active player focus moves from fine-grained scene play to broader spatial or narrative scope, or from broader scope back into fine-grained play.

## Examples

Submap to Locus: leaving a tavern scene and entering the town-level act.

Locus to Realm: completing local investigation and traveling to the capital.

Realm to Region: resolving a political chapter and entering regional war posture.

Region to World: triggering a plague, war, technological revolution, or magical cataclysm.

World to Submap: manifesting a global condition as a specific scene detail: quarantined gates, missing iron tools, refugee column, empty temple, closed market.

## Required Transition Packet

A transition packet includes:

- source partition;
- destination partition;
- route or montage type;
- minimum and maximum elapsed time;
- resources spent;
- encounter/event table checks;
- NPC/background tick advancement;
- flags promoted upward;
- flags propagated downward;
- world conditions manifested locally;
- anchors required for the next scene.

## Rule

The Fiction Constructor may narrate the transition, but the engine determines the transition.

## Compression Modes

Explicit mode: play each moment at spotlight/scene scale.

Travel montage: compress path movement while resolving encounters, supplies, and scheduled events.

Downtime montage: compress training, recovery, crafting, research, faction work, and relationship actions.

Absence summary: summarize what changed while the player or troupe was away.

World manifestation: translate arc/epic conditions into local details.

## Constraints

The engine cannot compress through unresolved mandatory consequences.

The engine cannot skip a scheduled event that intersects the active route or destination unless a policy explicitly defers it.

The engine cannot let a lower-scope action rewrite world state without propagation thresholds or plot triggers.

The engine cannot let world scope dictate a player choice; it can only alter affordances and consequences.
