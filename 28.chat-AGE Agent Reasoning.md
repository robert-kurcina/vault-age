# 3️⃣ The “Future State” Question

When I asked:

> Should mutation proposals reference future state?

What I meant was:

Should an agent be allowed to reason like:

“If I strike him, that will anger his allies, which will destabilize the court, which will weaken my reign…”

That is predictive simulation across partitions.

There are three ways to handle this.

---

## Option A — No Future Simulation

Agents reason only from current committed state.

They act locally.

Consequences propagate after commit.

Pros:

- Cheap
    
- Stable
    
- Scalable
    

Cons:

- Less strategic depth
    

This is what most games do.

---

## Option B — Controlled Local Projection

Agent can simulate within:

- Its own partition
    
- Limited depth
    
- Limited scope
    

Example:  
A king considers court reaction (court partition),  
but not geopolitical consequences (higher partition).

That’s manageable.

---

## Option C — Recursive Cross-Partition Projection

Full-blown:  
“I simulate you simulating me simulating you…”

This explodes combinatorially.

This is expensive and unstable unless tightly bounded.

And you’re right to be cautious.
